Banku recipe

Banku is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from fermented corn and cassava dough. The dough is kneaded and cooked in hot water until it forms a smooth, thick paste with a slightly sour taste.

It's typically shaped into small balls or portions and served alongside soups, stews, or grilled fish. Banku is a staple food in Ghana and parts of West Africa, known for its unique texture and ability to complement various sauces and proteins. CopyRetry

Ingredients

  • Corn dough (fermented corn meal)
  • Water
  • Salt
